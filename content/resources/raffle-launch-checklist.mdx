## The 60-Point Raffle Launch Checklist

Used internally on every first-time raffle engagement we run. Work through it from top to bottom. If any line isn't clearly checked off by your target launch date, delay the launch — almost always worth it.

### Phase 1 — Program foundation (Weeks 1–3)

1. Executive sponsor named and engaged weekly.
2. Raffle operating lead named; 20+ hours/week committed.
3. Board approval documented for the program, budget, and use of proceeds.
4. Cause-to-ticket-buyer message drafted and socialized internally.
5. Format chosen (50/50, Catch the Ace, calendar, prize-driven) with documented rationale.
6. Cadence chosen (monthly, bi-weekly, continuous, single-cycle).
7. Jurisdictional scope defined (provincial, state, cross-border).
8. Preliminary revenue model built with realistic assumptions.
9. Marketing budget approved (typically 15–25% of projected gross).
10. Use-of-proceeds plan aligned with board-approved programs.

### Phase 2 — Licensing & regulatory (Weeks 2–14)

11. Charitable eligibility confirmed for the target jurisdiction.
12. Organizational documentation gathered (bylaws, financials, directors).
13. Platform selected AND confirmed AGCO/state-registered in your jurisdictions.
14. License application drafted against the program design.
15. Prize insurance quote obtained where required.
16. Use-of-proceeds budget attached to application with line items.
17. Draw mechanics documented step-by-step including RNG specification.
18. Financial controls documented (bank separation, reconciliation cadence).
19. Application submitted.
20. Follow-up contact established with regulator.
21. Approval received and documented.

### Phase 3 — Platform & payments (Weeks 6–14)

22. Platform contract signed; SLA reviewed.
23. Ticket product/SKU structure configured.
24. Prize architecture tiers configured (grand, early-bird, secondary).
25. Payment processor integrated and tested at volume.
26. Apple Pay / Google Pay verified on multiple devices.
27. Tax and surcharge rules configured where applicable.
28. Refund / error path tested end-to-end.
29. Checkout flow tested on iPhone, Android, desktop, tablet.
30. Ticket confirmation email tested and branded.
31. Audit log and reporting dashboards validated.

### Phase 4 — Brand, creative & marketing (Weeks 8–16)

32. Program name and logo finalized.
33. Landing page live on production domain with SSL.
34. Landing page tested for Core Web Vitals and Lighthouse score.
35. Hero creative and secondary creative produced (photo and motion).
36. Email templates designed and coded responsively.
37. SMS copy drafted and compliance-reviewed (CASL / TCPA).
38. Pre-launch email sequence scheduled (3–5 sends).
39. Paid social creative (5+ variants) produced and campaign-structured.
40. Local PR list drafted; milestone press releases pre-written ($1M, $5M, final week).
41. Radio / billboard plan approved if used.

### Phase 5 — Operations & support (Weeks 10–16)

42. Customer support staffing plan in place for peak hours.
43. Support knowledge base / FAQ written.
44. Escalation path defined for regulatory or technical issues.
45. Draw-day runbook written and dry-run completed.
46. Winner notification template approved.
47. Prize payout procedure documented.
48. Crisis communications plan drafted (what if the platform goes down during a peak window).

### Phase 6 — Launch & first 72 hours (Launch week)

49. Final pre-launch all-hands walk-through completed.
50. Pre-launch teaser email sent.
51. Launch-day email sent at optimal time zone.
52. Launch-day social push across all channels.
53. Local press notified of launch.
54. First-24-hour revenue reviewed against forecast.
55. Checkout error rate monitored (target under 1%).
56. Support response time monitored (target under 10 minutes for chat).
57. First press milestone release drafted for the first expected threshold.
58. First-week retrospective scheduled.
59. Daily jackpot tracking and publicly-visible ticker functioning.
60. Post-cycle review framework prepared ahead of cycle close.
