## Prize Architecture Worksheet

A structured exercise for designing or optimizing your prize tier structure. Work through the 8 sections in order. Each section has a specific decision to make and a heuristic for how to make it.

---

### Section 1 — Your baseline expectation

Before designing prizes, write down a realistic revenue expectation for this cycle.

- **Expected total ticket sales**: $________
- **Expected total prize payout (50% for 50/50)**: $________
- **Net proceeds target**: $________

**Heuristic:** first-cycle programs should assume 30–60% of what peers with similar supporter bases achieve. Do not project based on the best case.

---

### Section 2 — Grand prize anchor

For 50/50: the grand prize is 50% of the jackpot — non-negotiable.

For Catch the Ace: the grand prize is the progressive pot — grows with each week the Ace isn't caught.

For prize raffles: the grand prize is fixed. Critical decision: what exact prize?

**Heuristic for prize raffles:** pick a prize with cultural weight in your community. A Jeep for a rural / outdoors community beats a generic "luxury prize package." A home beats a car for an upwardly mobile urban community.

---

### Section 3 — Early-bird prizes

Structure: one or more smaller prizes awarded to tickets purchased before a specific threshold.

**Recommended:** at least one early-bird prize. Programs without them consistently underperform on early-cycle revenue.

**Timing decision:** At what point do you draw the early-bird? Options:

- **Date-based**: "All tickets purchased by [date]."
- **Threshold-based**: "First X tickets sold."
- **Hybrid**: "Tickets purchased before X date OR first Y tickets, whichever comes first."

**Heuristic:** aim for the early-bird deadline to hit at approximately 25–35% of your cycle. Hit too early (under 20%) and you leave momentum on the table; hit too late (over 45%) and the incentive doesn't pull buyers forward.

**Amount decision:** how much to offer as an early-bird prize?

**Heuristic:** target 2–5% of expected gross revenue as the early-bird prize amount. Split across 1 (large impact) or 2–3 (broader reach) winners.

---

### Section 4 — Secondary prizes

Additional prizes awarded mid-cycle or at milestone thresholds.

**Options:**

- A secondary prize at a specific ticket-sales milestone (e.g., "When we hit $500K in ticket sales").
- A recurring weekly bonus prize during active weeks.
- A specific mid-cycle draw date.

**Heuristic:** one well-placed secondary prize draws more mid-cycle revenue than two or three small ones. Resist the urge to add many.

---

### Section 5 — Bonus / final-week prizes

Prizes designed specifically to incentivize final-week and final-day ticket purchases.

**Options:**

- "Final 72 hours" bonus draw.
- "Draw day only" additional prize pool.
- Progressive bonus that grows with late-cycle sales.

**Heuristic:** a final-week bonus of 1–2% of expected total revenue typically returns 3–4x in additional ticket velocity during the final push.

---

### Section 6 — Total prize architecture budget

Sum of all prizes (grand + early-bird + secondary + bonus).

For 50/50: grand is by definition 50% of jackpot. Everything else is additive from operating margin.

For prize raffles: all prizes are fixed; sum them up and confirm you can cover them.

**Overall heuristic:** additional prize architecture (beyond grand) typically consumes 4–10% of gross revenue. More than 12% and you're giving away margin that should go to the cause.

---

### Section 7 — Draw mechanics

How each prize is drawn. Specificity matters for both regulatory compliance and supporter trust.

For each prize:

- **Which tickets are eligible?** (All purchased tickets, tickets purchased before [date], ticket numbers ending in specific digits, etc.)
- **When is the draw?** (Specific date/time or triggered by a milestone?)
- **Who conducts the draw?** (Executive director, board chair, independent auditor?)
- **How is it documented?** (Video, auditor signature, witness log?)

**Heuristic:** over-document. Every draw should have a paper trail you'd be comfortable sharing with a regulator two years later.

---

### Section 8 — Regulatory approval

Every prize tier must be specified in your license application. The AGCO (or equivalent regulator) will review the full prize structure, not just the grand prize.

- [ ] All prizes documented with exact amounts or prize descriptions.
- [ ] Draw mechanics for each prize clearly specified.
- [ ] Prize insurance or reserve confirmed for fixed-value prizes.
- [ ] License application reflects the final approved prize architecture.

**Warning:** changing prize architecture after license approval requires notification and sometimes re-approval. Get it right before submitting.

---

## Typical full architecture (for a mid-sized 50/50)

- **Grand prize**: 50% of jackpot (by definition)
- **Early-bird**: 1–3% of expected revenue, drawn at ~30% of cycle
- **Secondary milestone**: 1–2% of expected revenue, at a specific ticket-sales milestone
- **Final-week bonus**: 1–2% of expected revenue

**Total non-jackpot prize cost**: approximately 4–7% of gross revenue.
