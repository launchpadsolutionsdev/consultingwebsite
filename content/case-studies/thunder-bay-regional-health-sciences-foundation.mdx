## The Challenge

By 2019, Thunder Bay Regional Health Sciences Foundation had been running a raffle program for several years. The results were respectable but unspectacular — cycles closing in the low hundreds of thousands. The hospital's capital needs, however, were growing faster than traditional fundraising could keep up with.

The board's question: could the 50/50 become something structurally different — a primary revenue stream for the foundation, not a supplementary one?

## The Approach

The program was rebuilt from the ground up. Not the platform — the program. Every lever that had been "good enough" was re-examined against a question: *what would this look like if this program were the #1 revenue line for the foundation?*

**Prize architecture.** Moved from a single grand-prize structure to a tiered system with early-bird prizes at ticket-sales milestones and secondary bonus draws. Every tier was modeled against expected ticket-buyer behavior; nothing was set "by vibes."

**Marketing cadence.** Built out a weekly marketing calendar: email every Monday and Friday, daily social creative, paid Meta ads geographically targeted to Northwestern Ontario first, expanding outward as the jackpot grew. Local radio added in the final two weeks of each cycle.

**Press strategy.** Every jackpot milestone — $1M, $5M, $10M — became a pre-planned press moment, with news releases drafted in advance and local reporters briefed ahead of each threshold.

**Customer support.** Staffed humans (not bots) on chat, email, and phone during peak sales hours. During the final 72 hours of a cycle, coverage escalated to near-continuous.

**Iteration discipline.** After every cycle, a formal retrospective: revenue-by-hour, traffic source performance, email deliverability, prize tier conversion. One or two things changed for the next cycle; the rest held constant.

The program didn't get there in one cycle. It got there through roughly 20 cycles of incremental improvement, with each cycle building on the last. The record-breaking month was the compound interest of four years of operational discipline.

Beyond the headline number, what's grown is the sustained baseline: the foundation now has a raffle program that reliably produces multi-million-dollar cycles, even without record-breaking peaks. The variance has narrowed; the floor has risen.

## What Made It Work

**1. Emotional anchor.** Thunder Bay Regional Health Sciences Foundation serves the entire Northwestern Ontario region. Virtually every family in the catchment has a connection to the hospital. That emotional resonance is the foundation every cycle has been built on.

**2. Operational rigor.** Nothing about what the team does is secret. The difference is that they actually do it — every cycle, without letting standards slip.

**3. Press leverage.** The cumulative effect of appearing in CTV, CBC, TB Newswatch, and national outlets at every milestone created a self-reinforcing brand gravity that smaller programs can't replicate.

**4. Patient iteration.** The team never chased magic bullets. Every cycle improved on the last by a few percentage points. The compounding arithmetic did the rest.

## The Takeaway

For any hospital foundation, regional charity, or service organization evaluating a raffle program: the Thunder Bay trajectory is achievable. Not the exact dollar number — that's a function of population, emotional resonance, and long-term investment — but the structural approach is portable.

The preconditions:
- An emotionally resonant cause with a defined community.
- A multi-year commitment to operational discipline, not a one-cycle experiment.
- A team (in-house or partner) with real charitable gaming experience.
- A board willing to invest in marketing the way you would for a product launch.

When those conditions are met, the results follow.
