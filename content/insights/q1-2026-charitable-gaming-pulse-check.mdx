Welcome to our inaugural quarterly Pulse Check — an honest, data-driven look at what's actually moving the needle across our client portfolio, distilled for charity leadership, board members, and other industry operators.

We'll publish one of these every quarter. Expect specificity, not hype. Where we don't have clean data yet, we'll tell you.

## Executive summary

- **Raffle revenue is up Q1 YoY** across our portfolio, with the median client program +14% vs. Q1 2025.
- **SMS adoption has reached critical mass** — two-thirds of client programs now run at least one compliant SMS campaign per cycle.
- **Subscription tickets are the surprise of the quarter** — clients that added recurring-purchase options are seeing meaningful lift at near-zero marginal CAC.
- **Prize architecture remains under-optimized** at the median program. There's more revenue to be unlocked here than in any other single lever.
- **Cycle-length optimization is becoming a real conversation.** Monthly 50/50s are no longer always the right answer.

## 2. The SMS story

Two years ago, fewer than 15% of our client programs were running active SMS campaigns. As of Q1 2026, the number is 66%.

**Why now?** Three factors converged:
1. Platform SMS capabilities matured to the point of being easy to integrate.
2. CASL/CAN-SPAM compliance best practices are now standardized.
3. Early-adopter results forced late-adopter FOMO.

**What's working in SMS:**
- 1–2 messages per cycle, not weekly.
- Timed to specific high-value moments: final 48 hours, jackpot milestones, winner announcements.
- Mobile-first link destinations — if they land on a slow page, you've wasted the message.

**What's not working:**
- High-frequency SMS ("cycle updates" every few days). Opt-out rates spike.
- Generic marketing copy. SMS rewards conversational, urgent, specific.

## 3. Subscriptions: the surprise

Several of our clients enabled recurring-purchase subscriptions (auto-ticket purchases every cycle) in the second half of 2025. Q1 2026 is the first quarter with meaningful data.

The data is encouraging:
- **12–28% of cycle revenue** now attributable to subscription purchases at client programs that have rolled this out.
- Subscribers show **4–6x** the LTV of single-cycle ticket buyers.
- CAC on subscription conversions is near-zero (conversion happens within existing buyer funnel).

**Implementation matters.** Programs that simply added a "subscribe" checkbox to checkout see modest uptake. Programs that built dedicated subscriber onboarding, communications, and perks see meaningfully higher adoption.

We expect this to be the most consequential operational change of 2026 for mature programs.

## 4. Prize architecture: the underutilized lever

We ran a specific analysis this quarter on prize architecture across our portfolio. The finding: **most programs are leaving 20–35% of revenue on the table through suboptimal prize structures**.

The most common errors:
- Flat grand-prize-only raffles (no early-bird or tier structure).
- Early-bird prize thresholds set too low — hit in week 1 with no momentum to build on.
- Secondary prize amounts set arbitrarily, not against modeled ticket-buyer value elasticity.
- No bonus prize structure for final-week purchases.

Programs that ran prize architecture optimization in the past 6 months saw an average **+22% revenue uplift** on the cycle following the change. That's revenue that was available on the previous structure but went uncaptured.

**Recommendation for any program director reading this:** review your prize architecture ahead of your next cycle. If you're not tier-structured with thoughtful early-bird and secondary prize thresholds, you're almost certainly leaving money on the table.

## 5. The cycle-length question

Historically, charity 50/50s have been monthly. Our data is starting to challenge that assumption.

At the top end of program maturity, we're seeing strong results from **bi-weekly and continuous** cycles — programs that never really "close" in the traditional sense and instead have rolling weekly draws.

**Caveats:**
- Shorter cycles only work at scale. Small programs (sub-$100K per cycle) lose the momentum benefit of jackpot buildup.
- Shorter cycles require mature operations. The weekly cadence demands are real.
- Marketing must be retooled. Monthly-cycle creative doesn't translate to bi-weekly.

For mature programs, this is worth a serious conversation. For earlier-stage programs, keep running the monthly cycle and tighten the other levers first.

## 6. What's not working

Honesty check — not everything is up and to the right. Several patterns are clearly underperforming:

**Generic "charity auction" platforms used for raffles.** Purpose-built raffle platforms are meaningfully outperforming multi-purpose platforms in our data. If you're running on a general-purpose fundraising platform and wondering why your numbers are flat, that's a candidate cause.

**Facebook-only marketing.** Programs relying primarily on organic social posts without paid, email, or PR stacked alongside are plateauing at very low revenue levels.

**Prize insurance without a clear cost/benefit analysis.** Several clients we've reviewed are paying for prize insurance coverage that mathematically makes no sense for their jackpot range. Worth reviewing annually.

**"Set and forget" cadence.** Programs that run the exact same cycle structure, marketing, and prize architecture quarter after quarter are the ones plateauing. The winning pattern is incremental iteration, every cycle.

## 7. Looking ahead: what we're watching in Q2

Our research focus for Q2 2026:

1. **AI-driven creative.** Several clients are piloting AI-generated email creative, landing page copy, and ad variations. Early results are mixed. We'll have a more substantive read in Q2.
2. **Cross-border pilots.** As U.S. state legislation progresses, a handful of Canadian charities are exploring structured cross-border programs. We'll report on what we're seeing.
3. **Subscription psychology.** How does subscriber behavior evolve over 3, 6, 12 cycles? When do subscribers churn? What reactivation tactics work?
4. **Full-stack SMS programs.** We expect the "SMS as primary channel, not supplementary channel" conversation to intensify.
